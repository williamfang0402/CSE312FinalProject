SECTION 02
what can we assume?

Algorithmic bias is not just a technical error, but a reflection of patterns learned from socially shaped data. Even when sensitive attributes are removed, AI systems can rely on proxy signals that reproduce similar inequalities. This means bias can emerge indirectly through structure rather than explicit intent. As a result, “neutral” models may still produce unfair outcomes.
